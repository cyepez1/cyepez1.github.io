# Design Philosophy — cairoyepez.com

Aesthetic reference for the site. Read before making design decisions.
Guest contributors read this before building their section.

## Structure
The site is a small multipurpose building. Each page is a room. The building has a
consistent material palette — the same walls, the same light — but each room is
furnished differently for its purpose. Coherence is structural; variation is expected.

Depth is built in. Surface pages read at a glance. Substance accumulates below — in
subpages, footnotes, linked sections, and things only visible to people who look.

## Visual register
The site exists at the intersection of print culture and early digital. Its references
are physical: zine production, newsprint, relief printing, card catalogs, institutional
archives, CRT monitors, physical media. These are not aesthetic moods applied from the
outside — they are the actual materials and processes the work thinks about.

The palette is warm and muted. The type is editorial. The grain is real. Nothing should
read as a template.

What this is not: flat design, gradient interfaces, framework defaults, infinite
scroll, autoplay, engagement mechanics, neumorphism, minimalist sans-serif monoculture.

## Influences
Four named reference points that set the atmospheric range. These are not
surface-level citations — they describe how the site thinks.

Mexican printmaking — Posada, Taller de Gráfica Popular.
Relief printing: woodcut, linocut, engraving. Bold ink on cheap paper. Broadsheet
mastheads. The calavera as a form of serious social commentary. This tradition is the
direct ancestor of zine culture and the masthead aesthetic. It also explains why the
site's editorial voice can be cutting without being decorative about it.

Bloodborne.
Gas-lamp gothic. Architecture built from obsessive ornament and institutional dread.
Scholars who pushed too far. The atmosphere here is not horror for its own sake — it
is what serious academic and archival aesthetics look like when taken to their logical
conclusion. Gravity. Weight. The sense that the material in the building matters.

Remedios Varo.
Alchemical surrealism. Figures operating arcane machinery in warm, precise detail.
Workshops, towers, instruments that are also spells. A sepia-ochre palette that maps
almost exactly onto this site's tokens. The connection between technical precision and
something stranger underneath.

Sofía Bassi.
Self-taught Mexican surrealist. Dream-continents, oceanic and cosmic landscapes, the
subconscious rendered in jewel tones. The deeper and stranger register — the material
that lives below the waterline.

## Per-page character
Each page inherits the palette and type system. Layout, density, and atmosphere are
specific to the medium.

| Page | Character |
|---|---|
| Index | Arrival. CRT hero, masthead, newspaper-format digest. |
| Blog | Long-form reading space. Dense type, archival structure. |
| Games | Wiki density. Card catalog logic. Computer lab energy. |
| Photos | Contact sheets. Chicago subject matter. Darkroom feel. |
| Music | Physical media. Liner note typography. |
| Portfolio | Technical. Blueprint and drafting table. (Formerly "Designs".) |
| Misc | Junk drawer. Corkboard. Things that don't fit elsewhere. |
| Now | Ephemera. Current state. Collage rather than grid. |
| Civic | Commentary and urbanism. Editorial register. |
| Bird page (future) | Field journal. Back porch. Observational. |

## Guest contributors
Future contributors build a room in the building, not a separate building.

Palette and type are inherited from `tokens.css`. Non-negotiable.
Layout within those constraints is theirs to propose.
Voice is their own, but the building has a register — nothing that breaks it.
Every guest section gets a consistent nameplate header: name, discipline, date.
(Component built once, reused.)

## Hard NOs
CSS frameworks without explicit prior agreement
Flat design, gradient blobs, neumorphism
Autoplay, infinite scroll, engagement mechanics
Fabricated prose or editorial voice (Cairo writes all of it)
Over-explanation, onboarding tooltips, spoon-feeding
Placeholder content beyond lorem ipsum and plausible titles
