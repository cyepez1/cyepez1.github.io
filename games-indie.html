Indie

Small studios. Massive hearts. These games punch far above their weight — proof that vision beats budget.

// the collection — chronological order

Terraria
2D sandbox with no ceiling

Terraria

2011

★★★★★

Hundreds of hours deep and still finding something new. Dig down, build up, fight everything, never stop. The best $10 ever spent.

Hotline Miami
Top-down ultraviolence. Miami 1989.

Hotline Miami

2012

★★★★★

A psychedelic fever dream about violence, identity, and the 1980s. The soundtrack alone earns a spot in a time capsule.

Hotline Miami 2:
Wrong Number
The story expands. Ends in fire.

Hotline Miami 2

2015

★★★★☆

Harder, louder, sadder. Larger levels, multiple perspectives, and an ending that still haunts. Not for the faint of heart.

Stardew Valley
One man built this paradise

Stardew Valley

2016

★★★★★

Farm, fish, befriend, fall in love. Eric Barone built this game alone. It shows in every pixel — pure, uncompromised creative vision.

Slay the Spire
Roguelike deck-builder. One more run.

Slay the Spire

2019

★★★★★

Invented a genre. Every run is a puzzle, every death a lesson. The Watcher alone has more depth than most full games.

Hades
Escape Tartarus. Die trying. Repeat.

Hades

2020

★★★★★

Supergiant's magnum opus. The narrative deepens with every failed escape. Characters you mourn, combat that sings. A perfect game.

Balatro
Poker + roguelike. Pure addiction.

Balatro

2024

★★★★★

One developer. Poker hands, jokers, and a number-brain that never shuts up. Impossible to put down. Arrived and conquered immediately.
