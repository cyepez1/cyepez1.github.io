Open World

Vast lands, brutal bosses, quiet sunsets on a hillside. Games that swallow you whole and spit you out changed.

// the collection — chronological order

Fallout:
New Vegas
The Mojave Wasteland. Wild West RPG.

Fallout: New Vegas

2010

★★★★★

The writing. The factions. The moral weight of every choice. New Vegas is still the high-water mark for open-world RPGs twenty years later.

Bloodborne
Yharnam bleeds at midnight

Bloodborne

2015

★★★★★

Gothic horror and cosmic dread wrapped in the tightest, most aggressive Soulslike FromSoftware ever made. No shields. No mercy.

No Man's Sky
Infinite procedural universe

No Man's Sky

2016

★★★★☆

The greatest redemption arc in gaming. What launched broken became a living galaxy. Explore planets, build bases, chart the unknown.

Dark Souls 3
The Age of Fire fades at last

Dark Souls 3

2016

★★★★★

A grand farewell to the Souls trilogy — interconnected legacy, blistering boss fights, and a melancholy that seeps into the bones.

Red Dead
Redemption 2
Dying West. Arthur Morgan lives.

Red Dead Redemption 2

2018

★★★★★

The most detailed open world ever made, anchored by the best cowboy story ever told. Arthur Morgan is a character study dressed as a video game.

Subnautica:
Below Zero
Alien ocean survival on Planet 4546B

Subnautica: Below Zero

2021

★★★★☆

Claustrophobic deep-sea tension with moments of breathtaking alien beauty. The ocean is not safe. You are very small.

Elden Ring
The Lands Between — George R.R. Martin

Elden Ring

2022

★★★★★

FromSoftware's masterwork. Open-world exploration married to the precision of Souls combat. Malenia alone could make this list.

Sons of
the Forest
Cannibal island survival horror

Sons of the Forest

2023

★★★☆☆

Base-building survival on a gorgeous, terrifying island. Kelvin and Virginia as companions are worth the price of admission alone.

Lords of
the Fallen
Dual realms — living and dead

Lords of the Fallen

2023

★★★★☆

The dual-realm Umbral mechanic is one of the most creative Soulslike ideas in years. Brutally difficult, visually stunning, deeply underrated.
